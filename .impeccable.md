## Design Context

### Users
Tahoe is used by a small content-production operator/team that needs to move one live topic into a publishable Chinese article-and-image package or a client-facing commercial content deliverable. The interface is used during daily editorial work, not as a marketing website.

### Brand Personality
Restrained, editorial, operational. Tahoe should feel like a focused production desk: clear hierarchy, quiet surfaces, and direct next actions.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a paper-like editorial workspace with fine dividers, compact controls, and minimal decoration. Avoid decorative card grids, glowing gradients, rounded novelty shapes, large icon tiles, and generic AI dashboard styling.

### Design Principles
1. Show the next useful action before explaining the whole workflow.
2. Prefer flat sections, rows, and dividers over stacked cards.
3. Use typography, spacing, and line rhythm for hierarchy instead of shadows or glow.
4. Keep controls compact and explicit; no decorative UI that does not help production.
5. Preserve Tahoe's backend contracts and artifact-first workflow while simplifying the visual layer.
