# Tahoe Business Model

> Last updated: 2026-04-23 by Codex
> This document defines Tahoe's operating flow, revenue logic, and strategic edge.

## Product Definition

Tahoe is not primarily an "AI content tool".

Tahoe should be operated as a **content operating system** for two linked businesses:

1. **Owned-media publishing**
2. **Client-service delivery**

Its job is to take one topic and turn it into a publishable article-and-image package with consistent quality, then reuse that same production method for paid client work.

## Core Operating Flow

Tahoe's near-term operating flow should stay narrow:

1. **Direction setup**
   - define account role, audience, voice, and topic boundary
2. **Topic judgment**
   - decide what is worth covering and what angle is worth taking
3. **Draft production**
   - generate title, lead, master draft, image brief, and publish packaging
4. **Quality review**
   - judge whether the draft is worth publishing, not just whether it is complete
5. **Image production**
   - turn approved image briefs into refined static images
6. **Publishing and review**
   - publish, observe response, and feed winning patterns back into the system

This is the minimum loop Tahoe must make stable before video returns as a main production target.

## Revenue Model

Tahoe should operate on a dual-engine revenue model.

### Engine 1: Owned-Media Revenue

Owned-media accounts are not only content channels. They are also:

- proof that Tahoe's workflow produces quality
- acquisition channels for future clients
- living case studies for the service business

Near-term owned-media revenue can come from:

- platform content revenue
- ad / sponsorship deals
- content commercialization
- future paid reports / paid communities / light consulting

### Engine 2: Service Revenue

Service revenue is the more reliable near-term cash engine.

Tahoe should support three service layers:

1. **Content production service**
   - topic planning
   - article writing
   - packaging
   - static image production
2. **Editorial system service**
   - account planning
   - column design
   - content operating frameworks
3. **Technical delivery service**
   - internal AI content workflows
   - industry intelligence + writing systems
   - multi-account content production tooling

## Strategic Edge

Tahoe should not try to win on breadth.

Tahoe's edge should be built around three narrower advantages:

### 1. Judgment, not just generation

Most AI tools can generate text.

Tahoe should become better at:

- deciding what is worth covering
- choosing the right angle
- detecting weak drafts before publication

This is why review seams, audience panels, and artifact readiness checks matter.

### 2. Text-image consistency

Tahoe should not stop at copy.

The output should feel publishable because:

- the article has a clear judgment
- the package feels intentional
- the static images match the article's tone and focus

### 3. Reusable account method

Tahoe's value is not one article.

Tahoe should be able to repeatedly answer:

- how one account should sound
- how one topic family should be covered
- how one channel should package and publish

That method can later be reused for clients.

## Account Roles

The three current owned-media accounts should not all behave like generic news feeds.

They need distinct editorial jobs.

### 1. AI快讯

- **Role**: AI information filter
- **Reader promise**: "I help you catch what changed in AI today and why it matters."
- **Primary content**:
  - model launches
  - agent/product updates
  - major platform moves
  - short industry implications
- **Voice**:
  - fast
  - clear
  - product-aware
  - low-hype

### 2. 全球股市

- **Role**: market-variable interpreter
- **Reader promise**: "I help you see what global markets are really reacting to."
- **Primary content**:
  - US / HK / CN market moves
  - earnings and company reactions
  - macro signals
  - risk and sentiment shifts
- **Voice**:
  - calm
  - direct
  - high-density
  - non-promotional

### 3. 消费时尚

- **Role**: brand and aesthetic judgment editor
- **Reader promise**: "I help you understand what runway, brand, and consumer shifts actually mean."
- **Primary content**:
  - runway interpretation
  - brand moves
  - product and styling signals
  - consumer mood and aesthetic trend shifts
- **Voice**:
  - sharp
  - tasteful
  - brand-aware
  - not empty "shopping-guide" language

This third line should explicitly avoid becoming a generic shopping or trend-aggregation account.

## Current Article Direction

Every account should have a stable article job:

- **AI快讯**: explain the change
- **全球股市**: explain the variable
- **消费时尚**: explain the signal

That means the output is not just "news".

It is:

- filtered
- structured
- judged
- made publishable

## What Tahoe Must Not Become

Tahoe should avoid these traps:

- a broad "everything content" dashboard
- a low-quality full-automation factory
- a generic SaaS before the method is proven
- three owned-media accounts that all sound like the same news bot

## 30-Day Priorities

### Priority 1: Stabilize the article package loop

Make this flow reliable:

- topic -> brief -> master draft -> title/package -> image brief -> image run -> publish package

### Priority 2: Sharpen account voice

Each account needs:

- clearer role
- clearer article angle
- clearer title style
- clearer "what we never sound like"

### Priority 3: Improve review quality

The review layer should answer:

- is this publishable
- what is weak
- what should be revised first

### Priority 4: Build service-facing reuse

Start translating owned-media methods into client-service structures:

- reusable briefs
- reusable content packages
- reusable review standards

## Success Signals

Tahoe is working if:

- one topic can quickly turn into a publishable article-and-image package
- the three accounts sound clearly different
- owned-media output can be reused as proof for paid service work
- quality improves through feedback, not just through more model calls
